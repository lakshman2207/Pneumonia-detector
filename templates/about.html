What is Pneumonia?

Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs. The air sacs may fill with fluid or pus, leading to symptoms like cough, fever, chills, and difficulty breathing. It can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi.

Causes & Risk Factors

  • Bacteria: Streptococcus pneumoniae
  • Viruses: Influenza, COVID-19
  • Fungi: In immunocompromised patients
  • High risk: Infants, elderly, smokers, people with chronic conditions

Common Symptoms

  • Chest pain when breathing or coughing
  • Persistent cough (may produce phlegm)
  • Fever, chills, sweating
  • Fatigue and shortness of breath
  • Nausea or vomiting

Types of Pneumonia

There are different forms, such as:

  • Community-acquired: From daily exposure
  • Hospital-acquired: From prolonged stays or ventilators
  • Aspiration: From inhaling food, liquid, or vomit
